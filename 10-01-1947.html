Indian Aviation Accidents

Accident: January 10, 1947

Aircraft Type: Douglas C-47A-25-DK (DC-3 in civilian form)

Location: Korangi Creek, Karachi (then part of British India, now Pakistan)

Crew Members:

  • Typically operated with 3 to 5 crew members, including a pilot, co-pilot, and possibly a flight engineer or radio operator.

Cause of the Accident: The aircraft was destroyed during its approach to land. The exact cause remains unclear, but possible contributing factors include:

  • Adverse Weather Conditions: Potential low visibility or coastal conditions.
  • Pilot Error: Navigational challenges common at the time.
  • Mechanical Failure: Less common with the DC-3, but not impossible.

Description: The aircraft was reportedly destroyed, making it a total loss. This crash is part of early Indian and later Pakistani aviation history, as military aircraft were used in civilian roles during this period of transition.
